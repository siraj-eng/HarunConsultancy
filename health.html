Course Overview

Health and Medical Sciences represent one of humanity's most vital professions, focusing on disease prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and the promotion of well-being. This field combines scientific knowledge with compassionate care to improve and save lives.

As global health challenges evolve and healthcare systems expand, the demand for skilled medical professionals continues to grow. This field requires dedication, empathy, scientific aptitude, and a lifelong commitment to learning and service.

Main Medical Branches

Medicine & Surgery

Comprehensive medical training to become licensed physicians who diagnose, treat, and manage diseases across all specialties.

  • Medical Doctor (MD)
  • Surgeon
  • Medical Officer
  • Consultant Physician
Duration: 6-7 years + internship

Nursing

Holistic patient care, clinical support, health education, and community health promotion across all healthcare settings.

  • Registered Nurse
  • Nurse Practitioner
  • Midwife
  • Nurse Educator
Duration: 3-4 years + registration

Clinical Medicine

Training for clinical officers who provide medical care, diagnosis, and treatment in hospitals and community health centers.

  • Clinical Officer
  • Medical Assistant
  • Surgical Assistant
  • Anesthesia Assistant
Duration: 3-4 years + internship

Pharmacy

Study of medications, their effects, formulation, dispensing, and management of pharmaceutical services.

  • Pharmacist
  • Pharmaceutical Scientist
  • Drug Inspector
  • Hospital Pharmacist
Duration: 4-5 years + internship

Public Health

Focus on population health, disease prevention, health policy, epidemiology, and community health management.

  • Public Health Officer
  • Epidemiologist
  • Health Educator
  • Health Policy Analyst
Duration: 4 years

Dentistry

Diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of oral diseases, dental surgery, and oral health promotion.

  • Dentist
  • Oral Surgeon
  • Orthodontist
  • Dental Public Health
Duration: 5-6 years

Career Opportunities

Healthcare professionals serve in diverse settings with excellent progression:

Medical Doctor

Diagnose and treat illnesses, manage patient care, and provide medical leadership in healthcare settings.

Salary: Ksh 150,000 - 1,000,000+
Registered Nurse

Provide patient care, administer medications, monitor health status, and support patients and families.

Salary: Ksh 50,000 - 400,000+
Clinical Officer

Provide primary healthcare, diagnose common illnesses, and manage treatment in clinical settings.

Salary: Ksh 60,000 - 350,000+
Pharmacist

Dispense medications, provide drug information, ensure safe medication use, and manage pharmacy services.

Salary: Ksh 70,000 - 500,000+
Public Health Officer

Develop health programs, monitor disease outbreaks, and promote community health initiatives.

Salary: Ksh 55,000 - 400,000+
Lab Technologist

Conduct medical tests, analyze samples, and provide diagnostic information for patient care.

Salary: Ksh 45,000 - 300,000+

Work Settings: Hospitals, Clinics, Community Health Centers, Research Institutions, Pharmaceutical Companies, Public Health Organizations, NGOs, International Health Agencies, and Private Practice.

Is Healthcare Right for You?

This noble profession calls for individuals who:

  • Have genuine compassion for others
  • Excel in biology and sciences
  • Can handle pressure and emergencies
  • Are detail-oriented and meticulous
  • Have strong communication skills
  • Value lifelong learning
  • Can work long and irregular hours
  • Maintain professionalism under stress

The Healthcare Professional: Medical careers require emotional resilience, scientific aptitude, and unwavering commitment to ethical practice. The rewards include job security, meaningful work, continuous learning, and the profound satisfaction of making a difference in people's lives.
